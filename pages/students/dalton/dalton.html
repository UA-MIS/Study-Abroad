A Class, Adventure, and Great Learning Experience - Small Town Alabama to Spain

Well that's.... interesting. Dalton

I was eager to explore Spain, one of the first things I noticed was a strong commitment to renewable energy and clean technology. The country is clearly focused on efficiency, utilizing mass public transportation and technology to better the average persons day. The experience of navigating a new culture, especially one where I didn't speak the language, was both challenging and rewarding. I struggled at first with ordering food and buying things, but quickly learned some phrases, embraced the countries navigation system and started using google translate a bit too much.

The Innovations of Spain....

Spain seamlessly integrates technology into daily life, making it incredibly convenient for both residents and tourists. One standout innovation is the use of QR codes in restaurants and cafes. You can scan a code at your table to view the menu, order, and pay directly from your phone, helping us non-natives with translations and taking the hardest part out of ordering food if you don't count choosing where to eat. Contactless payment methods are also widespread, allowing quick payments where the employee never takes your card out of sight. Spain's metro and train systems are highly efficient, one of our guides said you can reach almost any destination within Madrid in under 30 minutes. The country's commitment to sustainability is shown with electric buses, trains, and even ubers (electric ubers were way cheaper than combustion engine alternatives). These innovations make navigating and enjoying Spain's cities easier and more enjoyable, enabling someone who knows near zero Spanish to get around and enjoy the country.

What Stood Out to Me

Spain's commitment to renewable energy was clear, on the train we saw fields of solar panels and wind turbines, along with with one nuclear power plant. This focus on clean energy goes alongside the efficiency seen elsewhere in Spain, this shift towards clean energy seems to be more recent as smaller and less modern areas were still based almost solely on carbon fuels. What truly stood out to me, though, was the incredible diversity of food. The cities are a melting pot of foods from various cultures. I loved the variety, but my favorite was the Spanish Omelette. The Spanish Omelette was one of the simplest dishes that nearly every restaurant served, it will soon be cooked in Alabama (hopefully we will do it justice)! The ability to see and experience such different cultures within such a small time-frame was truly a once in a lifetime experience.
