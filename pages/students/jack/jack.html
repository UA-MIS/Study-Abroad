Information Technology in Spain

Our trip to Spain gave us the opportunity to talk to many IT professionals. The most memorable of these was our talk with Alex Lozano, a professor of cyber intelligence at the Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona and an expert in Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) methodologies. Professor Lozano opened my eyes to the value that OSINT can provide to organizations. He showed us how vast amounts of open source information can be analyzed and made sense of. This information can be used by companies and organizations to make important decisions. I was astonished, however, when Professor Lozano showed us an analytics tool called Maltego. Using Maltego, he was able to search entire social media platforms for people using name and facial recognition powered by AI. He acknowledged the threat that this poses to personal privacy and security and warned us to be careful of the information we put online. Overall, the lecture opened my eyes to the importance and power of data analytics.

Madrid

The first city we visited on our trip was Spain’s capital, Madrid. The city is filled with beautiful buildings all connected by easily walkable streets. There are many plazas scattered throughout the city where people congregate. I was amazed at how clean the city was and how nice the people were. While we visited the many historical sites in Madrid, we frequently used the metro to move around the city. The efficiency and organization of this technology was very impressive. My favorite part of our time in Madrid was visiting the Royal Palace of Madrid and the Cathedral. The architecture and attention to detail on these buildings was stunning. We were able to climb to the top of the Cathedral where we had a breathtaking view of the city. Madrid was one of the most beautiful cities I have ever visited, and I would love to go back!

Barcelona

After a week in Madrid, we boarded a high-speed train and traveled to Barcelona. In Barcelona, we walked down narrow streets full of the gothic architecture that exists all over the city. We visited the Sagrada Familia, a massive church that has been under construction for 140 years. I was impressed with the age of the city and the many different types of architecture that exist in it. We saw many beautiful views of the city during our stay. The most memorable of these was from a hill in the middle of the city that we hiked up. Once at the top, we saw a 360-degree view of the city. I thoroughly enjoyed my time in Barcelona, and I will never forget its distinct personality.
