My Adventure
By: Sam Riddle in Spain

Landlocked

21 May 2024

This page is a short summary of my trip to Spain as part of the UA MIS Study Abroad Program for Summer 2024. My tenure in Spain lasted from May 6, 2024 until May 21, 2024, during which I got to visit two cities, Barcelona and Madrid. As a part of the program, I had the chance to experience Spain from an academic perspective as well as a traveler's perspective, which encouraged me to think critically about what was I experiencing. These academic observations as well as my personal reflections are captured in the following images, quotes, and paragraphs.

      Final Reflection

      I feel fortunate to have been able to participate in this study abroad program. When Professor Jeff Lucas initially informed me about the trip, I hesitated. Not only had I never been abroad before, but also I had never really considered studying abroad before, just because I hadn't conceived how valuable of an experience traveling abroad would be. When I finally stepped out of my comfort zone and accepted the challenges of traveling abroad, I was pleasantly surprised. Turns out, an international trip can be both fun and relaxing.

      From Left to Right: Chase, Carson, and Sam
      — Barcelona, Spain

      It's hard to convey with words the wonder I experienced on this trip. The best words I can find are not my own; being a Kansas native, it is almost impossible to ignore how fitting L. Frank Baum's novel The Wizard of Oz is in describing my reaction to setting foot onto Spanish soil.

      Toto, we're not in Kansas anymore.
      — Dorothy Gale, Wizard of Oz

      My "teleportation" to Spain was indeed quite shocking. However, in the spirit of Dorothy Gale and the lesson she learned, this shock was arguably good for me, because it pushed me out of my comfort zone. The title of this page, "Landlocked", is not just in reference to Kansas geography; it also refers to my state of mind pre-Spain. I simply didn't know what the outside world was like before I went to Spain, so I didn't expect for there to be anything worth discovering abroad. Even though I grew up landlocked both literally and mentally, after this voyage across the Atlantic, my closed perspective about the outside world was cracked open.

      I don't know yet what the ramifications of my shifting perspective are. As of now, I'm not sure if I want to keep traveling abroad. However, with time, perhaps my perspective will change again.
